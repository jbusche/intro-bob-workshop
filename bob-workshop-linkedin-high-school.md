# LinkedIn Workshop for High School Students
## Build a Profile That Helps You Get a Job

### Workshop Overview
This hands-on workshop helps high school students create or improve a LinkedIn profile they can use for jobs, internships, volunteer roles, clubs, and future opportunities. Students will learn how to present themselves professionally online, write about their skills and experiences, and leave with a profile they can keep improving over time.

**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Age Group:** High School (grades 9-12)  
**Prerequisites:** Basic computer literacy, an email account, and access to LinkedIn  
**Materials Needed:** Laptop, internet connection, and a short list of student activities/experiences

---

## Workshop Goals

By the end of this workshop, students will:
- Understand what LinkedIn is and why employers use it
- Create or improve the key sections of a LinkedIn profile
- Write a strong headline and summary
- Add experience, activities, skills, and education
- Leave with a profile that is ready to share with employers or mentors

---

## Why LinkedIn Matters

LinkedIn is more than a social media platform. It is an online professional profile that can help students:

- Show employers they are serious and responsible
- Highlight school, volunteer, club, sports, and work experience
- Share skills and interests
- Find part-time jobs, internships, and networking opportunities
- Start building a professional presence before college or full-time work

### Key Message for Students
You do **not** need to have a full-time job history to make a strong LinkedIn profile. Employers also care about:
- Volunteer work
- Babysitting or lawn care
- School projects
- Clubs and leadership roles
- Sports and team activities
- Creative work
- Certifications and training
- Community involvement

## 30-Minute Workshop Plan

### Part 1: Quick Introduction (5 minutes)

#### What to Explain
Introduce LinkedIn as a professional tool for opportunity, not just a place for adults with careers.

#### Discussion Prompts
- Where do employers look when deciding who to interview?
- What would you want someone to know about you in 30 seconds?
- What have you already done that shows responsibility, teamwork, or initiative?

#### Instructor Talking Points
- A LinkedIn profile is like a living digital resume
- It can grow with you over time
- Start now, even if your experience is mostly from school and activities
- Keep everything truthful, clear, and professional

---

### Part 2: Build the Core Profile (20 minutes)

Students should work directly in LinkedIn while following these steps.

#### Step 1: Add a Professional Photo (3 minutes)

**Goal:** Create a strong first impression

**Checklist:**
- Use a clear photo of your face
- Smile or look friendly and confident
- Choose a simple background
- Avoid filters, heavy editing, sunglasses, or group photos
- Dress neatly

**If students do not have a photo today:**
- Leave it for later
- Do not let the lack of a photo stop progress on the profile

---

#### Step 2: Write a Strong Headline (4 minutes)

Your headline appears under your name and should quickly describe who you are and what you are interested in.

**Weak example:**
- Student

**Stronger examples:**
- High School Student Interested in Business, Marketing, and Customer Service
- Student Athlete | Team Player | Interested in Sports Management and Leadership
- High School Student Seeking Part-Time Job Opportunities | Reliable, Organized, and Hardworking
- Aspiring Graphic Designer | High School Student with Creativity and Digital Design Skills

**Simple formula:**
`[Who you are] + [interests or strengths] + [what you're looking for]`

**Student task:**
Write a headline in one sentence.

---

#### Step 3: Write the “About” Section (6 minutes)

This section is a short introduction. It should sound professional but still feel natural.

**Prompt for students:**
Write 3-5 sentences that answer:
- Who are you?
- What are you involved in?
- What skills or strengths do you have?
- What kinds of opportunities interest you?

**Example About section:**
I am a high school student who is interested in learning, building new skills, and gaining real-world work experience. Through school, volunteering, and extracurricular activities, I have developed strengths in communication, teamwork, and responsibility. I enjoy helping others, staying organized, and taking initiative when working on projects. I am interested in part-time jobs and future opportunities where I can continue to grow professionally.

**Helpful tip:**
Students do not need to sound overly formal. Clear and honest is better than trying to sound impressive.

---

#### Step 4: Add Education and Experience (5 minutes)

**Education section:**
Students should add:
- Their high school
- Expected graduation year
- Relevant classes, pathways, or programs if helpful

**Experience section:**
Remind students that “experience” can include unpaid and school-based work.

**Examples of experience they can add:**
- Babysitting
- Yard work
- Pet sitting
- Volunteer roles
- Tutoring
- School leadership
- Team captain
- Club officer
- Helping with family business
- Fundraising events
- School projects with real responsibilities

**Strong bullet point examples:**
- Helped supervise and care for children after school and on weekends
- Worked with team members to organize club events and activities
- Volunteered at community events and assisted with setup, greeting, and cleanup
- Managed time effectively while balancing school, sports, and part-time responsibilities

**Tip:**
Focus on responsibilities, not just titles.

---

#### Step 5: Add Skills (2 minutes)

Students should add 5-10 skills that match their real strengths.

**Example skills:**
- Communication
- Teamwork
- Time Management
- Leadership
- Customer Service
- Organization
- Problem Solving
- Public Speaking
- Microsoft Office
- Google Workspace
- Social Media
- Graphic Design
- Writing

**Reminder:**
Choose skills honestly. Employers value accuracy.

---

### Part 3: Final Polish and Reflection (5 minutes)

#### Quick Review Checklist
Students should check:
- Does the profile have a clear headline?
- Is the About section complete?
- Is education listed?
- Is at least one experience or activity included?
- Are skills added?
- Does the profile look professional and accurate?

#### Reflection Questions
- What section was easiest to write?
- What was hardest?
- What is one thing you can add later to make your profile stronger?
- How could this profile help you apply for a job, internship, or volunteer role?

## Suggested Instructor Script

### Opening
“Today you are going to build something useful for your future: a LinkedIn profile you can use to help get a job, internship, or other opportunity. Even if you have never had a formal job before, you already have experiences and skills that matter.”

### During the Workshop
“Do not worry about making it perfect today. The goal is to create a strong first version. Your profile can keep growing as you gain more experience.”

### Closing
“Your LinkedIn profile is a tool that helps other people understand your strengths. Keep updating it as you join activities, gain experience, and learn new skills.”

---

## Support for Students Who Get Stuck

### If a student says, “I don’t have any experience”
Help them think about:
- Times they helped family or neighbors
- Volunteer work
- School projects
- Clubs, sports, music, theater, or leadership
- Times they solved problems, helped others, or showed responsibility

### If a student says, “I don’t know what to write”
Use sentence starters:
- I am a high school student interested in...
- I have built skills in...
- Through school and activities, I have learned...
- I am looking for opportunities to...

### If a student says, “My life isn’t impressive”
Remind them:
- Employers are not expecting a long resume from a high school student
- Effort, reliability, attitude, and willingness to learn matter a lot
- Everyone starts somewhere

## Optional Extension Activities

If time allows or for follow-up homework, students can:
- Customize their LinkedIn URL
- Connect with teachers, coaches, mentors, or supervisors
- Add volunteer experiences and clubs
- Ask a teacher or mentor to review their profile
- Search for part-time jobs, internships, or local opportunities
- Add certifications, awards, or projects

---

## Best Practices for High School Students

### Do's ✅
- Be honest
- Be professional
- Use correct spelling and grammar
- Highlight real strengths and experiences
- Keep improving your profile over time

### Don'ts ❌
- Do not exaggerate
- Do not post unprofessional photos
- Do not copy someone else’s summary word-for-word
- Do not include private information you should not share publicly
- Do not leave your profile empty if you can add school and activities

Students can copy and adapt this:

**Headline:**  
High School Student Seeking Part-Time Job Opportunities | Responsible, Friendly, and Hardworking

**About:**  
I am a high school student who is motivated to learn, grow, and gain work experience. I have developed skills in communication, teamwork, and responsibility through school, extracurricular activities, and helping in my community. I am dependable, organized, and ready to contribute in a positive way. I am interested in part-time work and future opportunities that will help me build professional experience.

## Instructor Notes

### What Success Looks Like
By the end of 30 minutes, most students should have:
- A LinkedIn account or profile started
- A headline written
- An About section drafted
- Education added
- At least one experience, activity, or volunteer item listed
- Several skills added

### Facilitation Tips
- Normalize that students may feel awkward writing about themselves
- Encourage students to help each other brainstorm experiences
- Walk around and help students turn vague experiences into professional language
- Keep the pace moving so everyone finishes a basic profile
- Emphasize progress over perfection

### Safety and Privacy Notes
- Students should avoid sharing private contact details publicly
- They should use professional and school-appropriate language
- If students are under 18, remind them to review privacy settings carefully
- Encourage students to ask a parent, teacher, or counselor if they are unsure what to share

---

## Wrap-Up

Building a LinkedIn profile is a practical first step toward getting a job and exploring future opportunities. Students do not need years of experience to begin. A clear, honest, and professional profile can help them stand out and grow their confidence.

**Start with what you have, show what you can do, and keep building from there.**
